2D ANIMATION

2D Animation

The art of generating movement in a two-dimensional environment is known as 2D animation. This covers backdrops, creatures, FX, and characters.The popular and versatile medium of 2D animation is experiencing a huge resurgence. It is widely used in TV shows, video games, movies, commercials, mobile apps, and websites.From pencil and paper hand-drawn processes to the use of digital approaches using computer tools like Toon Boom Harmony or Adobe After Effects, 2D creation has developed.

To create a 2D animation, a large number of images are assembled into a sequence that gives the impression of movement and action. "The technique of filming successive drawings or positions of puppets or models to create the appearance of movement when the movie is exhibited as a sequence" is how these kinds of animation are described.

Unlike in 3D animation, objects in 2D animation move up, down, left, and right but not toward or away from the camera.2D animation is divided into three parts. They are pre-production,production and post production. Each of them is divided into many catergories.

History

2D Animation - Moving Image

The evolution of animation

2D Animation Timeline

2D animation was developed in the late 1800s, It is a time-honored animation method. In the past, artists have traditionally drawn each frame using pencils before painting the designs onto cells, which are sheets of clear plastic. These cells were later each individually imaged.

Drawing a picture, then drawing another one near to it while keeping the change very slight is the history of 2D animation. To build a scenario, hundreds of drawings were done, starting with the first and ending with the last. To get the desired outcome, this method is then demonstrated in a prompt sequence.These days, we have more suitable computer software, which has helped to simplify this process. 2D animation makes it possible to illustrate ideas in more detail and creativity. Basically, 2D animation has advanced significantly. From Fantasmagorie in 1908 up until the present, there have been many cartoons and flash animations.

Techniques

There are many techniques used in 2D animation. Some of them are Cut-out , Rotoscoping, Digital , Flash and many more. Firstly , The cut-out animation is a technique done using paper or other materials, flat, 2D characters and objects are created, then moved around on a flat surface. The animation is then produced by filming and replaying the actions. Secondly,Using Digital animation 2D animations are made with software. Using a mouse or digital drawing pad, the animator creates the characters and objects, and the program fills in the missing frames to produce the animation.

Further,Flash animation is a digital animation that makes use of Adobe Flash technology. Because Flash animation is simple to integrate in websites and has a tiny file size, it is frequently utilized for web-based animations.Moreover, In Rotoscoping, To generate a 2D animation using this method, live-action video is traced over frame by frame. Although it takes time, this method may produce incredibly lifelike movements. Finally,Traditional hand-drawn animation is the most popular method in 2D animation. To give the appearance of movement, each frame is manually drawn before being photographed one at a time and played back quickly.
